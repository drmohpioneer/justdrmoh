Tarek — the AI front-desk for clinics

طارق — مساعد استقبال ذكي للعيادة

A 24/7 bilingual AI receptionist that answers patients in real Egyptian Arabic and English, gives safe medical guidance, and books appointments automatically — designed and safety-tuned by a practicing cardiologist.

Open the live demo →
⏳ The demo runs on a free server — give it up to a minute to wake up on the first click.
The problem

Clinics lose patients in the gaps

A question at 11pm. "What do I do before the test?" "Do I even need to come in?" When no one answers, the patient books somewhere else. Front-desk staff can't be online around the clock — and most clinic chatbots are worse than nothing: they either invent dangerous medical advice or can't actually do anything useful.

The solution

An AI receptionist that actually works — safely

Tarek talks to patients in natural Egyptian Arabic and English, around the clock. He answers heart-health questions, explains things in plain language, gives safe first-aid guidance and flags clearly when a patient should head to the ER, and books appointments straight into the clinic's Google Sheet. No app to download, no extra staff to hire.

✓Answers FAQs 24/7Hours, location, services, prep instructions — instantly.
✓Plain-language medical helpExplains conditions & medications in real Egyptian Arabic.
✓Safe triage & escalationFlags red-flag symptoms → "see a doctor now."
✓Books appointmentsCaptures the booking into Google Sheets automatically.
✓Fully bilingualSwitches between Arabic and English seamlessly.
✓Secure by designNo exposed keys, least-privilege, filtered output.
Why it's not "just a GPT wrapper"

Built to a professional security standard

Most AI bots are "vibe-coded" — the API key sits exposed in the patient's browser, there are no safety limits, and they're trivial to break. (A scan of 5,600 such apps found 2,000+ vulnerabilities and 400+ leaked keys.) Tarek is built on the secure backend-proxy pattern: the key never touches the patient's phone, every reply is filtered as plain text, and the bot can only do safe things — answer and book, nothing else. There's nothing for an attacker to steal and nothing dangerous for it to do.

Gemini — the brain Python backend proxy Google Sheets booking Render hosting

Designed, safety-tuned, and built by a practicing cardiologist — so the medical guidance is correct, the tone fits real patients, and the bot knows exactly when to stop and say "go see a doctor." That's the part a generic developer can't do.

Hakeem — the AI scribe that speaks your Arabic

حكيم — كاتب الكشف الذكي للطبيب المصري

An ambient AI scribe for Egyptian doctors. It listens to the consultation in real colloquial Arabic and writes the clinical note for you — while the patient's identity never leaves your computer. Currently in private trial on my own clinic visits.

📹 Full walkthrough video coming. It runs on real patient consultations, so there's no public click-to-try demo — here's exactly what it does instead.
The problem

Notes eat the doctor's day — and foreign tools can't do our Arabic

Every visit ends with paperwork. Western AI scribes exist, but they choke on Egyptian colloquial Arabic, send patient data to foreign clouds, and weren't built for how a doctor here actually talks and writes. So most of us still type notes by hand, after hours.

The solution

Speak the visit. Get the note.

Hakeem records the consultation, transcribes the messy spoken Arabic on the doctor's own machine, and structures it into a clean clinical note in the exact terse shorthand a cardiologist actually writes — in English and Arabic, plus a referral letter and a patient-friendly report. The doctor reviews, edits, and exports.

Why it's safe

The patient's identity never leaves the doctor's computer

This is the part that matters for medical data. The audio is transcribed locally; names and identifiers are stripped on the machine before anything is sent; only de-identified placeholder text reaches the cloud (on a no-training tier); and the real values are restored locally on the final note.

1 · Local transcriptionwhisper.cpp runs on the machine — the audio never leaves the computer.
2 · Identifiers stripped hereNames & IDs removed locally, before anything is sent.
3 · Only de-identified text sentThe cloud sees placeholder tokens, never the real identity — no-training tier.
4 · Real values restored locallyThe final note gets the real details back, on the doctor's machine.

Bedaya — a real starting point for anyone who wants to begin

بداية — أول خطوة لأي حد عايز يبدأ

A free Arabic web app for anyone starting a weight-loss journey. In two minutes it gives you your real numbers — what your body actually needs — and a plan built around the Egyptian food you already eat. Born from my own 25 kg journey, and left completely free.

Open Bedaya live →

The problem

Everyone starts with someone else's plan

People don't quit because losing weight is hard — they quit because they start with a plan built for someone else's body and someone else's food. It doesn't fit, it feels like punishment, and a week later they stop. And no one tells a beginner the one thing that actually matters: what does my body need?

The solution

Your numbers, your food, free

Bedaya asks a few questions in two minutes, then gives you your own numbers — your calories and protein — and a plan built on the food you really eat: foul, koshari, the things you order. It doesn't tell you to stop eating your food; it shows you how to make it work. And it's clear about its limits: it's a starting point, not a replacement for your doctor.

✓Your real numbersCalories & protein computed for your body — not a generic target.
✓Your Egyptian foodA plan on foul, koshari, what you order — not sad diet food.
✓Two minutesA few questions — no counting, no logging every bite.
✓Safety-firstFlags when to see your own doctor; never overrides medical care.
✓Works for eating outReal "what to order" guidance, not just home cooking.
✓Completely freeNo ads, no upsell — the tool I wished I'd had.
Why it's not "just a GPT wrapper"

The medical numbers are locked — the AI never touches them

The part that makes a health tool trustworthy: the calories and protein come from a fixed clinical engine, not the AI. The AI only arranges the food around those locked numbers — so it can never invent a wrong medical figure, no matter what a user types. On top of that: bot protection that doesn't punish real people, per-user limits, and no API key ever exposed in the browser.
