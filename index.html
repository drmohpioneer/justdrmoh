The problem

Clinics lose patients in the gaps

A question at 11pm. "What do I do before the test?" "Do I even need to come in?" When no one answers, the patient books somewhere else. Front-desk staff can't be online around the clock — and most clinic chatbots are worse than nothing: they either invent dangerous medical advice or can't actually do anything useful.

The solution

An AI receptionist that actually works — safely

Tarek talks to patients in natural Egyptian Arabic and English, around the clock. He answers heart-health questions, explains things in plain language, gives safe first-aid guidance and flags clearly when a patient should head to the ER, and books appointments straight into the clinic's Google Sheet. No app to download, no extra staff to hire.

Why it's not "just a GPT wrapper"

Built to a professional security standard

Most AI bots are "vibe-coded" — the API key sits exposed in the patient's browser, there are no safety limits, and they're trivial to break. (A scan of 5,600 such apps found 2,000+ vulnerabilities and 400+ leaked keys.) Tarek is built on the secure backend-proxy pattern: the key never touches the patient's phone, every reply is filtered as plain text, and the bot can only do safe things — answer and book, nothing else. There's nothing for an attacker to steal and nothing dangerous for it to do.
